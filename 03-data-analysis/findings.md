# Data Analysis Findings

## What was analysed

One year of first-line IT service desk calls from retail stores (July 2025 – June 2026), covering approximately 27,750 calls logged in TOPdesk. The data was cleaned and modelled in Power Query and analysed in Power BI to understand what drives store call volume and how much of it could be resolved through self-service.

## Key findings
- 68.8% of calls are deflectable, a documented fix exists, resolved on first call, no supplier needed, and not a physical fault.
- 86% already had a documented fix, yet stores still called — the knowledge base exists but isn't reaching staff. The gap is access, not content.
- 81% of store-and-fault pairs recurred , 2,264 were logged five or more times, one store alone logged 223 calls. The same predictable problems resurface constantly.
- Volume is concentrated, four of the top seven subcategories are password resets: simple, known-fix requests, not complex faults.
- Demand peaks in December, retail's busiest trading period, when store IT problems are most disruptive and analyst time is most stretched.

## Measurable impact
- 5,700 analyst hours a year spent on first-line store calls — the equivalent of ~3.5 full-time analysts.
- £99,000 a year in avoidable first-line cost from deflectable calls (at £8 per call).
- 2 full-time analysts' worth of work tied up on issues that already have a documented answer.

## What it points to

The demand is repetitive, predictable, and self-serviceable, but the existing knowledge base isn't reaching staff at the point of need. Writing more articles won't fix that. Notably, 41% of call descriptions are three words or fewer ("HHt not woking", "Scanner till 3") too vague for a menu-driven FAQ, but well suited to an AI assistant that understands natural language. This is the basis for the recommendation in the next stage.

## Honest limits

The data sizes the opportunity but can't explain why staff don't use the knowledge base, and first-call resolution proves an analyst fixed the call, not that a bot could. Both need stakeholder interviews before committing to a solution.
