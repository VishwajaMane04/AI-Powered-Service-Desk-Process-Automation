# Business Problem
The IT Service Desk receives around 450 to 600 tickets per week from retail stores. A large share of these are repetitive, first-line issues such as password resets, printer faults, POS terminal reboots, till scanner errors, card machine declines, and handheld device problems that store teams could resolve themselves.
The core problem is that documented fixes already exist for these issues. Step-by-step guides, FAQs, and known reboot or reset steps are available, yet the issues still reach the desk as tickets. Analysts then spend time walking store staff through solutions that have already been written down.
This creates three compounding costs. The first is wasted staff time and cost, as analyst hours are consumed by trivial, repeatable tasks. The second is slow resolution for stores, as staff wait in a ticket queue for issues that could be fixed in minutes. The third is reduced analyst productivity, as skilled analysts are tied up on low-value work instead of complex problems.
This project investigates which issues drive the ticket volume, how many are genuinely self-serviceable, and whether an AI-powered self-service solution could deflect them, reducing ticket volume, cost, and resolution time.
